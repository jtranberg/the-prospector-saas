# Data Sources

TheProspector combines publicly available hockey information, licensed data where applicable, organization-generated scouting intelligence, and AI-assisted enrichment to create a comprehensive hockey intelligence platform.

The platform is designed around transparency regarding where information originates and how it is used.

---

# Data Philosophy

Information is most valuable when its origin is understood.

The Prospector strives to:

* Clearly identify information sources
* Respect intellectual property
* Maintain data transparency
* Improve data quality through validation
* Separate original platform content from third-party information

---

# Public Hockey Information

Player profiles may include information that is publicly available through recognized hockey organizations and publications.

Examples include:

* Player names
* Positions
* Nationalities
* Teams
* Leagues
* Seasons
* Statistics
* Jersey numbers
* Heights and weights (where available)
* Player profile links

Public information is organized to improve research efficiency and user experience.

---

# Licensed Data

Where appropriate, the platform may integrate data obtained through authorized APIs, commercial agreements, or licensed partnerships.

Licensed providers retain ownership of their respective content and trademarks.

Integration with licensed services is performed in accordance with applicable agreements.

---

# Organization Data

Organizations using The Prospector may create proprietary information including:

* Scouting reports
* Player evaluations
* Rankings
* Watch lists
* Internal comments
* Prospect tags
* Development observations

Unless intentionally shared, these records remain private to the organization that created them.

---

# User-Generated Content

Users may contribute information through platform features such as:

* Notes
* Rankings
* Tags
* Saved searches
* Watch lists
* Workflow preferences

Users remain responsible for the accuracy and appropriateness of information they create.

---

# AI-Assisted Enrichment

Artificial intelligence may assist in organizing, summarizing, and enriching player information.

AI-generated content is intended to:

* Improve research efficiency
* Highlight relationships
* Organize information
* Support scouting workflows

AI-generated information should always be reviewed by qualified hockey personnel before being relied upon for operational decisions.

---

# External Resources

Player profiles may include links to external resources to assist further research.

Examples may include:

* Official league websites
* Team websites
* Public player profiles
* Authorized data providers
* News articles

External websites operate independently and maintain their own policies and terms of use.

---

# Data Quality

The Prospector continuously improves data quality through:

* Validation
* Enrichment
* User feedback
* Error correction
* Platform updates
* Ongoing engineering improvements

Despite these efforts, information may occasionally become outdated or incomplete as players, teams, leagues, and statistics change over time.

Users should verify important information using official sources whenever appropriate.

---

# Intellectual Property

The Prospector organizes information but does not claim ownership of third-party player data, trademarks, league names, team names, or external content.

Original software, analytics, documentation, platform design, and proprietary features remain the intellectual property of App Intelligence unless otherwise stated.

The platform's data ecosystem continues to evolve.

Future enhancements may include:

* Additional licensed providers
* Expanded enrichment
